Short Term Impact

What changed after a few years after US imposed multiple sanctions?

Congress passes the Iran-Iraq Arms Nonproliferation Act of 1992, which prohibits the transfer of controlled goods or technology that might contribute “knowingly and materially” to Iran’s proliferation of advanced nuclear weapons.


Iran decides to increase uranium enrichment after several attacks on their nuclear sites.

A graph and a pie chart showing it's electricity and oil production in
relation to its energy sectors. Source: ResearchGate

After years of continuous failed diplomatic efforts to negotiate with Iran, European officials concluded that sanctions were necessary to get Iran to agree with its international obligations, including the Nuclear Non Proliferation Treaty. In 1996, the U.S. Congress passed the Iran-Libya Sanctions Act, which penalizes foreign and U.S. investment exceeding $20 million annually in Iran’s energy sector. Since 2007, Europe has been as involved as the United States in diplomatic efforts to secure U.N. sanctions on dual-use items that assist Iran’s nuclear and missile programs.
The International Atomic Energy Agency Board of Governors designed a resolution calling for Iran to suspend all enrichment and reprocessing. The resolution requires Iran to declare all material relevant to its uranium-enrichment program and allow the inspectors to conduct environmental sampling at any location.
