US Imposing Sanctions on Iran

The US imposed numerous sanctions on Iran for various different political reasons from 1979 until present day.

In response to Iran’s continued nuclear proliferatin activities, the United States and other countries imposed sanctions to restrict Iran and prevent its progress in prohibited nuclear activities. These sanctions were designed to regulate Iran’s prohibited nuclear and missile programs, directly or indirectly impact the Iranian economy in a manner that helps curb its nuclear activities; and encourages Iran to constructively engage in discussions with the countries that have imposed sanctions to fulfill its obligations. The sanctions had several impacts such as a declining trend of Iranian exports from oil products, inflation, economic recession, a plunging valuation of Iranian Rial, increased smuggling, rising unemployment, deteriorating trade relations with the neighboring countries, shortages of medical supplies and isolation from the international community.
