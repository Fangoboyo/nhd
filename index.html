In response to Iran's continued nuclear proliferation activities, the United States and other countries imposed sanctions to restrict Iran and prevent its progress in prohibited nuclear activities. These sanctions were designed to regulate Iran's prohibited nuclear and missile programs, directly or indirectly impact the Iranian economy in a manner that helps curb its nuclear activities; and encourages Iran to constructively engage in discussions with the countries that have imposed sanctions to fulfill its obligations. The sanctions had several impacts such as a declining trend of Iranian exports from oil products, inflation, economic recession, a plunging valuation of Iranian Rial, increased smuggling, rising unemployment, deteriorating trade relations with the neighboring countries, shortages of medical supplies and isolation from the international community.

Iran has continued to produce nuclear weapons to equip their army for War. Iran's nuclear production started to get out of hand, leading to the sanctions that minimized imports/exports, lowered the amount of nuclear weapons, and prompted other nations to stop oil trading with them. The starting of the sanctions was in 1979, following the U.S Embassy in Tehran. Several administrations had aided the U.S with the powerful sanctions.

But, what exactly are sanctions? Sanctions are mostly financial penalties that usually target the economy of a specific country. The sanctions may be imposed due to the excessive production of an item, in this case, the nuclear weapons constructed by Iran.
