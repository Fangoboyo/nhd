History of Iran

The Continued Nuclear Risks and Threats Made by Iran

The economic and social impact of nuclear proliferation in 1979 by Iran, a country with a long history of civil unrest, can have tragic and grave consequences to the community especially if it engages in a war and decides to use its nuclear strength.

A chart displaying the decline of oil exports since new sanctions were put out.

In response to Iran’s continued nuclear proliferation activities, the United States and other countries imposed sanctions to restrict Iran and prevent its progress on prohibited nuclear activities. These sanctions were designed to regulate Iran’s prohibited nuclear and missile programs, directly or indirectly impact the Iranian economy in a manner that helps curb its nuclear activities; and encourages Iran to constructively engage in discussions with the countries that have imposed sanctions to fulfill its obligations. The sanctions had several impacts such as a declining trend of Iranian exports from oil products, inflation, economic recession, a plunging valuation of Iranian Rial, increased smuggling, rising unemployment, deteriorating trade relations with the neighboring countries, shortages of medical supplies and isolation from the international community.

Iran has continued to produce nuclear weapons to equip their army for war. Iran’s nuclear production started to get out of hand, leading to the sanctions that ultimately minimized imports/exports, and prompted other nations to stop trading oil with them. The U.S. started imposing sanctions on Iran in 1979. Several administrations had aided the U.S. with these powerful sanctions.



But, what exactly are sanctions? Sanctions are mostly financial penalties aimed at causing negative impacts on the economy of the targeted country with the goal of changing their behavior that is threatening the global community. Sanctions could also be imposed on countries that have a relationship and aid the targeted country with the prohibited activities.
